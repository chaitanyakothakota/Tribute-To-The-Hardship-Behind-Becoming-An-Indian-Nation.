About Republic Day


  • Republic Day is a national holiday in India, celebrated on January 26th every year to commemorate the adoption of the Constitution of India. The Constitution of India came into effect on January 26, 1950, making India a republic.

  • The history of the Republic Day celebration in India can be traced back to the period between 1920s to 1940s when the Indian National Congress, under the leadership of Mahatma Gandhi, campaigned for the independence of India from British colonial rule. After India gained independence in 1947, a drafting committee was appointed to write a constitution for the new nation. The Constitution was adopted on November 26, 1949, and was implemented on January 26, 1950, making India a republic.

  • National Republic Day is a national holiday in India, celebrated on January 26th every year to commemorate the adoption of the Constitution of India. On this day, a grand parade is held in the national capital, New Delhi, featuring colorful floats representing different states of India, as well as performances by school children and a display of military might. sThe President of India also gives a speech on this day.

  • The main event of the day is held in the Rajpath, where a parade is held which is attended by dignitaries from all over the world along with general public. The parade starts from the Rashtrapati Bhavan and proceeds towards the India Gate. The parade showcases a display of India's cultural and military heritage. The President of India also gives a speech on this day. It is a day to honor the Constitution of India as it came into effect on January 26, 1950, making India a republic.

  • In addition to the parade, there are also cultural programs and competitions held in schools and colleges across the country to mark the occasion. People also hoist the national flag and sing the national anthem, and there are also patriotic songs and dances performed in various communities.

  • The celebration of Republic Day in India pays tribute to the hardship and sacrifice of those who worked to make India a sovereign nation. The Constitution of India, which came into effect on January 26, 1950, was the result of years of struggle and sacrifice by leaders and activists of the Indian independence movement.

  • The independence movement in India was led by figures such as Mahatma Gandhi, Jawaharlal Nehru, Sardar Patel, and many others who fought against British colonial rule through peaceful means of non-violent resistance and civil disobedience. They faced arrest, imprisonment, and violence at the hands of the British authorities but their relentless efforts led to the withdrawal of British colonial rule from India in 1947.

  • The adoption of the Constitution of India on January 26, 1950, marked the establishment of a democratic and secular republic, which guarantees the rights and freedoms of all its citizens. The celebration of Republic Day is therefore a tribute to the sacrifices and struggles of these leaders and the people of India, who worked to make India an independent and sovereign nation.
